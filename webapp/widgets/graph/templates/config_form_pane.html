The name given to the acquisition for future reference.

The sampling frequency of the graph. Availables values depends on the target devices capabilities

Decimation reduces the sampling frequency. A decimation of N means that 1 sample every N samples is acquired while all the other are discarded.
Effective Sampling Rate

The real sampling rate computed based on the selected sampling rate and decimation

When the trigger condition gets fulfilled for the given hold time, a trigger event happens.
This parameter control the position of the trigger event in the acquisition window. A value of 0% will put the trigger event on the left of the acquisition. 50% in the middle and 100% at the far right.
Useful to see before or after an event.

Timeout from the trigger event before the end of the acquisition. A value of 0 means "no timeout" and acquisition will finish once the buffer is full

Select what the X-Axis is.

  • Ideal time: Time deduced from the sampling frequency. Uniform sampling is assumed and uses no space in the datalogging buffer. Not available for variable frequency rates.
  • Measured time: Time measured by the device. Depending on the device configuration, sampling may or may not be uniform. Requires a 32bits slot in the datalogging buffer.
  • Watchable: Any watchable object given by the server. Drag & drop any Variable/Alias/RPV from a watch or list widget

The watchable object to use as the X-Axis. Any variable, alias or runtime published value (RPV) can be dropped in this field

Condition checked for trigger event.

  • Always true: Does not wait on any condition. Returns a graph as soon as the buffer is full
  • Equal: x1=x2
  • Not Equal: x1!=x2
  • Greater Than: x1>x2
  • Greater or Equal Than: x1>=x2
  • Less Than: x1<x2
  • Change More Than: Check the change between 2 samples of x1 to be greater than x2. The change dx1 must be in the same direction as the sign of x2
    |dx1|>|x2| & sign(dx1)=sign(x2)
  • Is Within: When x1 is close to x2 with a tolerance of x3.
    (|x1-x2|<|x3|)

The operand x1. Can be a constant or a watchable. Any variable, alias or runtime published value (RPV) can be dropped in this field

The operand x2. Can be a constant or a watchable. Any variable, alias or runtime published value (RPV) can be dropped in this field

The operand x3. Can be a constant or a watchable. Any variable, alias or runtime published value (RPV) can be dropped in this field

The amount of time, in milliseconds (fractional value possible), that the trigger condition must be true without interruption before launching the trigger event that will cause the acquisition to finish eventually.
